Founding Heroes

Mukhtar Auezov

Mukhtar Auezov (1897–1961) was a prominent Kazakh writer, public figure, and statesman who made a significant contribution to the culture and history of Kazakhstan. During the transition from the Soviet era to independence, he remained an influential figure. Auezov actively supported national identity, addressing important social and historical issues in his works. His literature inspired people to strive for independence and preserve national values. Through his literary contributions and engagement in public and political life, Auezov played a vital role in shaping Kazakhstan's national consciousness..

Dinmukhamed Kunaev

Dinmukhamed Kunaev (1912–1993) played a crucial role in Kazakhstan politics. Serving as the First Secretary of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of Kazakhstan during the period of perestroika, Kunaev supported the initiative for independence. In 1991, when Kazakhstan declared independence, Kunaev endorsed President Nursultan Nazarbayev's efforts, contributing to regional stability. His involvement in the events of 1991 and collaboration with the new authorities after the declaration of independence established him as a key figure in shaping the nascent Kazakhstani state.

Beyimbet Mailin

Beyimbet Mailin (1894-1937) was a prominent Kazakh statesman and cultural figure who played a significant role in shaping Kazakhstan's early 20th-century history. A staunch advocate for Kazakh national identity and cultural preservation, Mailin actively participated in the establishment of the Alash Autonomy, the first attempt at Kazakh self-governance in 1917. He also made significant contributions to the development of Kazakh literature and education. Throughout his career, Mailin remained committed to the interests of the Kazakh people, leaving a lasting impact on the nation's cultural and political landscape, helping shape its identity during a crucial period of change.

Alihan Bukeikhanov

Alihan Bukeikhanov (1866-1937) was a prominent Kazakh statesman and political figure who played a pivotal role in shaping Kazakhstan's early 20th-century political landscape. A staunch advocate for Kazakh national identity and cultural development, Bukeikhanov emerged as a leader during a period of significant political and social upheaval. He actively participated in the formation of the Alash Autonomy, the first attempt at Kazakh self-governance in 1917. Despite the challenges and setbacks faced by the Alash movement, Bukeikhanov's contributions to Kazakh national aspirations left a lasting impact on the country's history and development.
