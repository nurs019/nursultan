Independence of Kazakhstan (1991)

Kazakhstan declared its independence from the Soviet Union on December 16, 1991. This historic moment marked the starting point for the country's journey towards development and the formation of its own sovereign state.

Coat of Arms of Kazakhstan

The coat of arms of Kazakhstan was designed by Zhandarbek Malibekov and was adopted on June 4, 1992. The design symbolizes the rich cultural and historical heritage of Kazakhstan, with the steppe eagle representing freedom and power, the mythical horses embodying the nomadic spirit, and the shanyrak exemplifying the traditional nomadic dwelling. The coat of arms reflects the country's identity and aspirations, blending historical elements with contemporary symbolism.

Flag of Kazakhstan

The flag of Kazakhstan, adopted on June 4, 1992, symbolizes the nation's identity. The gold sun represents wealth and abundance, while the 32 rays signify progress and unity. The soaring golden steppe eagle embodies freedom and power. The national ornamental pattern "koshkar-muiz" on the hoist side is a tribute to the country's cultural heritage, introduced in 2006. The flag was designed by Shaken Niyazbekov and approved by Nursultan Nazarbayev, the first President of Kazakhstan, showcasing a collective effort to shape a meaningful national symbol.

Currency of Kazakhstan
