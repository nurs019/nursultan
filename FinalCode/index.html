Kazakhstan: A History of Conquest and Independence

Embark on a captivating journey through the rich and diverse history of Kazakhstan, tracing its evolution from the Neolithic era to its modern-day status as Central Asia's largest nation. Witness the domestication of the horse, the rise and fall of empires, and the unwavering spirit of the Kazakh people as they forge their independent identity.

Key Milestones

The Kazakh people faced numerous challenges in their pursuit of independence, including economic hardship, political repression, and cultural assimilation. However, their resilience and determination ultimately led to the realization of their aspirations. Key achievements on the path to independence included:

  • 1989: The formation of the Alash movement, which advocated for Kazakh autonomy and self-determination.
  • 1990: The declaration of Kazakhstan's sovereignty within the Soviet Union.
  • 1991: The adoption of the Declaration on State Sovereignty, marking the formal beginning of Kazakhstan's independent statehood.

Economic facts

  • Kazakhstan was one of the poorest republics of the Soviet Union.
  • The GDP per capita was only $1,500.
  • The main sectors of the economy were agriculture, mining, and manufacturing.
  • Kazakhstan was a major producer of grain, cotton, meat, and wool.
  • Oil, gas, and coal mining formed the basis of the manufacturing sector.

Path to Independence

  • 18th century: Russia's expansion into Central Asia led to the gradual incorporation of Kazakh territories into the Russian Empire.
  • 1917: Following the Russian Revolution, Kazakhstan experienced a period of political turmoil and unrest.
  • 1936: The Kazakh Soviet Socialist Republic was established within the Soviet Union, marking formal recognition of Kazakhstan as a distinct entity.
  • 1980s: Mikhail Gorbachev's reforms of glasnost and perestroika fueled nationalist sentiments in Kazakhstan, paving the way for independence.
  • 1991: Kazakhstan declared its independence from the Soviet Union, becoming the last republic to do so.
